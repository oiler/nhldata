## Findings

The matchup line signal shows teams like MTL, CGY, TBL, CAR, and MIN sending non-elite second/third-line centers into elite opponent territory. Jason Dickinson (CHI) at 47.6% is the most extreme case — he's not elite himself but facing elite forwards at nearly the same rate as Shane Pinto. Chicago barely has any elite forwards of their own, so Dickinson absorbs that burden regardless.

The top of this list is a mix of elite forwards doing their job and non-elite forwards who happen to be deployed on lines that draw tough matchups. MTL is the clearest team-level example: Suzuki (47.2%, non-elite) and Caufield (46.7%, elite) are on the same line and see the same opponents.

Note that MacKinnon sits 39th at 40.6% despite being one of the best players in the league. Colorado has enough talent that their elite forwards don't necessarily face the other team's elite forwards — opponents may not have a matchup line worth deploying against them.
