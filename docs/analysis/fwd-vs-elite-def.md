## Findings

Bryan Rust (52.2%) and Rickard Rakell (49.1%) at Pittsburgh are striking — both non-elite, but they skate with Crosby and draw the same elite defensive matchups he does. Auston Matthews (46.9%) appears as a "non-elite forward" here, which reflects that his numbers this season don't clear the 2.0 P/60 threshold despite the elite defensive attention he draws.

The sheltered elite forwards are also interesting — Beckett Sennecke (ANA, 32.3%) and Valeri Nichushkin (COL, 32.6%) are classified as elite forwards but rarely see elite opposing D. Nichushkin's COL deployment makes sense: MacKinnon draws the tough matchup, Nichushkin and Brock Nelson get easier assignments on the other lines.

The Pittsburgh effect is the clearest team story here. Crosby leads the league at 53.7% and his two primary linemates (Rust, Rakell) are 3rd and 11th overall — non-elite forwards drawing top-D matchups purely by association. This is the deployment elite concept working in reverse: the whole line gets targeted, not just the star.
