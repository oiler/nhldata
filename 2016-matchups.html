What is this?

This page provides a visualization of each NHL playoff matchup in hopes of offering a brief understanding on how each team compares in certain statistical categories.

How is it calculated?
  • All data is from Dec 1, through the end of the regular season
  • Each horiztonal bar (0-30) graphs where each team ranks — inside the assigned time period — among all 30 NHL teams. Left is bad, right is good.
  • The light gray lines inside each graph shows the average (mean) value for all current playoff teams (16) in that stat category
Why these stats?
  • We begin with Dec 1 because many who follow the game believe that teams begin to find themselves after the US Thanksgiving holiday. For more information, ask a Ducks or Habs fan.
  • There's a mix of process and result oriented stats here, presented with the intention to view together and tell a whole story, instead of seven different stories.
Where is the data from?

All data, with the exception of the PP and PK numbers, are from war-on-ice.com. Everything but PP & PK is 5on5 data. PP and PK data is from nhl.com (RIP).

What is not here?

Lots. These are not predictions. This is not intended to be an all-in-one guide, but instead just a resource to review matchup data that I don't see in other places. I threw this together quicky, in my spare time, and don't (yet) know D3 very well. The only other stat category I wish I could have included is a number that compared how distributed a team's goal scoring is across their lineup. Onto the matchups...
